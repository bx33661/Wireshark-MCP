## Triage

Explain what the capture mostly contains, identify the main communicators, and point to the three best next leads.

<Steps>
1. `wireshark_open_file`
2. `wireshark_quick_analysis`
3. `wireshark_stats_protocol_hierarchy`
4. `wireshark_stats_endpoints`
5. `wireshark_stats_conversations`
6. `wireshark_plot_traffic` or `wireshark_stats_io_graph` if timing matters
7. `wireshark_follow_stream` for the strongest lead
</Steps>

Deliver capture summary, top protocols, top talkers, obvious anomalies, and the three most useful filters, streams, or hosts to inspect next.

## Security

Determine whether the capture shows suspicious behavior and explain why. Treat `wireshark_stats_expert_info` as a lead generator, not proof of compromise.

Recommended confirmation tools:

- `wireshark_check_threats`
- `wireshark_extract_credentials`
- `wireshark_detect_port_scan`
- `wireshark_detect_dns_tunnel`
- `wireshark_analyze_suspicious_traffic`
- `wireshark_get_packet_details`
- `wireshark_get_packet_context`

## Incident response

Reconstruct what happened, when it happened, and which systems were involved. Separate capture boundaries from incident boundaries.

Deliver a timeline, affected hosts and services, IOC list, likely narrative, and containment or follow-up recommendations.

## Troubleshoot

Explain packet-level causes of slowness, failure, or instability. Compare client and server behavior before assigning root cause.

Use `wireshark_analyze_tcp_health`, `wireshark_stats_expert_info`, and `wireshark_stats_service_response_time`, then follow the problematic stream.

## CTF

Recover the flag or hidden payload while documenting the extraction path. Search obvious markers, inspect streams, decode verified payloads, and export objects when needed.

Deliver the recovered artifact, extraction path, exact evidence chain, and every decoding step.
